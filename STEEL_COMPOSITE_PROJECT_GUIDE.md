## Fixing Methods

### VHB Tape + Rivets (Recommended):

**VHB Tape**:
- 3M VHB 4991 or similar
- 25mm wide tape
- Continuous bond along channel length
- Provides primary attachment

**Rivets**:
- 4.8mm or 6.4mm diameter
- Spacing: 200-300mm along channel
- Provides backup/redundancy
- Prevents peel-off

**Why Both?**:
- VHB: Excellent shear strength, continuous bond
- Rivets: Backup, prevents edge lifting
- Combined: Professional, reliable system

### Rivet-Only (Budget):
- Closer spacing required (150mm)
- More penetrations in face
- Lower cost
- Adequate for most applications

## Post Attachment

### For Steel Composite Signs:

**Brackets** (recommended for larger signs):
- Steel brackets wrap around post
- Bolted through sign channels
- Capacity: 5-20 kN depending on design
- Professional appearance

**Jubilee Clips** (quick install):
- Stainless steel hose clamps
- 50-100mm diameter range
- Capacity: 0.5-2.0 kN per clip
- Use multiple clips
- Good for temporary or smaller signs

**Specialist Fixings**:
- Proprietary clamping systems
- Often jubilee-clip variants
- Follow manufacturer specs

## Common Questions

### Q: Can I use 600mm spacing to save cost?
**A**: Yes, structurally adequate for steel composite. But quality rating drops to "budget". Use 400mm for professional work.

### Q: Is steel composite heavier than ACM?
**A**: Yes, ~12 kg/mÂ² vs 5 kg/mÂ² for ACM. Consider in bracket/post design.

### Q: Does steel composite rust?
**A**: Faces are typically galvanized or coated steel. Edge sealing is important to prevent core corrosion.

### Q: Can I mix materials on one sign?
**A**: Not recommended. Stick to one panel type per sign for consistent performance.

### Q: What if wind pressure is higher than 828 Pa?
**A**: Run the calculation with actual pressure. May need closer spacing or thicker panel.
